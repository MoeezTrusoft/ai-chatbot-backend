## Editing Levels

We offer five distinct editing levels, priced per-word. Each level addresses a different stage of manuscript development. Most books benefit from a sequence — typically Manuscript Assessment → Developmental → Line → Copy → Proofreading — though many authors only need the later stages.

| Level | What it addresses | When to use it |
|---|---|---|
| **Manuscript Assessment** | High-level editorial critique with no in-text changes — strengths, weaknesses, structural recommendations | Earliest drafts, when deciding what kind of editing the manuscript needs |
| **Developmental Editing** | Big-picture work: plot, character, structure, pacing, argument coherence. Often substantial rewrites suggested | Early drafts; books with structural issues or narrative gaps |
| **Line Editing** | Sentence-level prose refinement — flow, rhythm, word choice, voice consistency | After developmental issues are resolved; before copy editing |
| **Copy Editing** | Grammar, syntax, punctuation, style-guide consistency, fact-checking of obvious claims | Standard for finished drafts; the editing most books need before publication |
| **Proofreading** | Final polish — typos, missed punctuation, formatting consistency, layout errors | After typesetting/formatting; the very last step before publishing |

We also offer **Substantive Editing** as a combined developmental+line edit and **Structural Editing** focused on chapter/section organization for non-fiction.

## What Influences Cost & Timeline

Per-word rates are the base. These complexity factors can adjust the total quote:

- **Manuscript condition** — Clean drafts edit faster than rough ones; very rough drafts may benefit from Manuscript Assessment first
- **Genre complexity** — Literary fiction with intricate prose, books with extensive made-up terminology, multi-POV narratives all add time
- **Multiple POVs/narrators** — Each requires distinct voice tracking
- **Technical/specialized content** — Medical, legal, scientific, financial content requires fact-checking and terminology accuracy
- **Non-standard formatting** — Verse novels, hybrid genres, books with extensive footnotes, mixed-media books
- **Extensive notes/comments needed** — Books needing heavy editorial guidance vs. light polishing
- **Author's first language** — Editing for ESL authors (still beautiful work, just different demands on the editor) takes longer
- **Writing style consistency** — Wildly inconsistent draft vs. consistent voice
- **Grammar/mechanics level** — Whether the edit needs to address fundamentals or just refinements

## Our Editing Process

1. **Manuscript evaluation & editorial assessment.** We start by reviewing your draft to understand its overall state — structure, clarity, areas of strength, areas needing work. You receive a recommendation on which editing level(s) will benefit your book the most. This step ensures we target the right level rather than over- or under-editing.

2. **Developmental editing (if needed).** For early drafts, we start with big-picture work — restructuring chapters, reordering sections, recommending scene additions or cuts, addressing pacing and flow at the story level.

3. **Line editing for style & clarity.** Once structure is sound, we refine prose line by line. Tone consistency, transitions, paragraph rhythm, sentence-level impact — all without losing your voice.

4. **Copy editing & proofreading.** Close-reading for grammar, spelling, punctuation, style-guide adherence, fact-checking obvious claims. We use Track Changes throughout. ***Nothing escapes this fine-toothed comb.***

5. **Author review & approval.** You receive the edited manuscript with all changes tracked. You accept, reject, or discuss each edit. We're happy to explain reasoning for any change you question. Together we finalize the text.

6. **Final proofreading pass.** After your accepted changes are integrated, our proofreader makes one last pass to catch anything that slipped through during revision.

7. **Final delivery.** A clean, polished manuscript file ready for formatting and publication. Free of errors, polished in style, consistent in voice — ready for cover design, formatting, and the world.
