# Catalog Transition & Rights Recovery Process

A catalog transition goes smoothly when the division of labor is clear and the tone is collaborative. BookCraft handles the operational work; the former publisher is only asked to provide information, in whatever order is convenient. There are no threats and no enforced deadlines — the goal is a clean, respectful close-out.

Authorization & Scope: Confirm the titles in scope and put in place your written authorization for BookCraft to act on your behalf with platforms, agencies, and the former publisher.

Information Request: Send a structured, section-by-section request to the former publisher covering rights, accounts, ISBNs and metadata, production assets, editorial and marketing materials, financials, third-party contacts, sub-licenses, and inventory.

Rights & Asset Verification: Review the responses, verify the chain of title, and confirm we have everything needed to continue each title without rebuilding from scratch.

Migration & Rebuild: Set up new accounts, transfer or register ISBNs, migrate listings to preserve continuity, rebuild metadata, and move and verify all files into secure storage under your imprint.

Reconciliation & Re-Launch: Complete the final royalty reconciliation, then optionally refresh and re-launch the catalog to re-engage readers.

Handover Sign-Off: Confirm in the records that the handover is complete, with a short closing acknowledgment from all parties.
