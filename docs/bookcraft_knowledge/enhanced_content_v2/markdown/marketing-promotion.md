## What Influences Cost & Timeline

Tier and duration set the base. These factors can shift effort:

- **Target audience size** — Niche audiences need more targeted (often less expensive) media; broad audiences need bigger budgets to break through
- **Genre competitiveness** — Romance, thriller, and fantasy are crowded; specialized non-fiction may be easier to dominate in its niche
- **Existing platform** — Strong author platform (large mailing list, social following) amplifies campaign ROI
- **Email list size** — A 10,000+ engaged list multiplies launch-day sales
- **Social following** — Existing reach reduces paid-ad dependency
- **Series vs. standalone** — Series benefit from reader-magnet (free book 1) strategies; standalones need stronger immediate hooks
- **Cover/blurb quality** — Strong cover/blurb make every dollar spent on ads more efficient (we may recommend cover updates before campaign launch)
- **Reviews/endorsements** — Books with 50+ Amazon reviews convert ad clicks dramatically better than books with under 10
- **Number of channels** — More channels = more management overhead but more diversified reach
- **Paid advertising level** — Ad spend is in addition to campaign management fees; budgeting is a campaign-planning conversation

## Our Marketing Process

1. **Strategic planning.** We establish your book's target audience, unique selling points, comp titles, and competitive positioning. We develop a tailored marketing plan and timeline aligned with your launch (or relaunch) goals. We assess your existing author platform — website, social, email — and identify foundational gaps to close before campaign starts.

2. **Pre-launch buzz & preparation.** Building anticipation in the months/weeks before release. Tasks include sending advance review copies to reviewers and bloggers via NetGalley and direct outreach, scheduling cover reveals and teaser content, growing reader engagement via social media and author newsletter, and securing endorsements or testimonials. *Goal:* have reviews and an audience ready on launch day.

3. **Launch campaign.** Coordinated multi-channel push during release week — press release, virtual launch events, aggressive social media, and initial advertising. If eligible, we leverage big promotional opportunities (BookBub Featured Deal, Amazon Vine eligibility, Apple Books featured placement) to drive a sales surge that improves rank and visibility for the rest of the campaign.

4. **Post-launch & ongoing promotion.** After launch, momentum-maintenance — soliciting more reviews, running discount promos at strategic intervals, podcast/blog tour engagement, ad campaign optimization based on data, and content marketing (blog posts, podcast guesting). The focus shifts to long-term brand building: maintaining an active author platform, nurturing a fan community, positioning for future releases.

5. **Performance review & iteration.** Weekly or monthly reporting on ad performance, sales attribution, review acquisition, and ranking. We iterate creative, audiences, and channel mix based on what's working. End-of-campaign report includes recommendations for the next phase.

## Common Questions

**What is included in your marketing and promotion service?**
Marketing tiers include strategic planning, pre-launch buzz building (ARCs, reviews, mailing list activation), launch-week campaigns (paid ads, PR, social), post-launch sustainment (review acquisition, ongoing ads, content), performance reporting, and at the higher tiers — influencer outreach, podcast tours, premium PR, and bookstore/library marketing. Specifics vary by tier.

**Do you offer email marketing services for book promotion?**
Yes. Email marketing is a foundational channel and part of every tier. We help build your list (lead magnets, signup incentives), develop welcome and launch sequences, integrate with major email platforms (Mailchimp, ConvertKit, Substack), and use email pushes during launch week. Many authors find their list is their highest-ROI channel.

**Do you offer social media management services?**
We provide social strategy, content templates, paid ad management, and influencer outreach as part of campaigns. We can also handle direct posting on your behalf as an extended service for clients who don't want to manage their own accounts. Most authors maintain their own organic presence (it's more authentic) while we handle the paid and outreach layers.

**Do you handle book blog tours for authors?**
Yes — Virtual Book Tour (10 stops) is an add-on that includes 10 blog and podcast features over a multi-week schedule. We handle outreach, scheduling, content prep (interview questions, guest posts), and follow-up. Larger tours (20+ stops) are scoped as custom campaigns.

**Can you help with crowdfunding campaigns for book promotion?**
Yes. Crowdfunding (Kickstarter, Indiegogo, Patreon) is a viable launch path for some books, especially illustrated editions, special-format releases, and series with established readerships. We help with campaign page design, video script and production, reward structure, and launch-week marketing.

**How do you handle negative reviews or publicity?**
Negative reviews are normal and not actionable individually. We focus on building a strong overall review profile (40+ reviews with a healthy distribution) so a few negative ones don't dominate. For negative publicity (controversy, factual disputes), we have crisis-response protocols — typically involving the author's direct, measured response and refraining from amplifying via marketing channels.

**How do you manage pre-order campaigns for upcoming books?**
Pre-order campaigns are highly effective for series authors and anticipated releases. We set up the pre-order on KDP and other retailers (typically 6 months before launch), build a pre-order incentive program (free bonus content, exclusive cover variants), drive pre-orders via email and social campaigns, and coordinate the launch-day push that converts pre-orders into rank.

**How do you ensure the marketing reaches international audiences?**
International marketing requires region-specific strategies. The International Promotion add-on covers UK, AU, CA, IN, and other markets. Each region has its own ad platforms (Amazon UK ≠ Amazon US), media outlets, and reader communities. For multi-region releases, we typically handle 2–3 regions per campaign rather than spreading thin globally.

**How do you help improve reader engagement for ongoing promotions?**
Ongoing engagement comes from content (blog posts, podcast appearances, author newsletter), strategic ad campaigns (retargeting past readers, lookalike audiences), and series-leverage (free book 1 to drive book 2-N sales). For evergreen titles, we run quarterly mini-campaigns rather than constant promotion.

**Do you provide weekly or monthly updates on marketing performance?**
Yes. Reporting cadence depends on your tier. Launch Essentials gets monthly summaries. Professional Campaign gets weekly performance reports during active campaign phases. Premium Blitz gets weekly reports plus a dedicated account manager for real-time questions. All tiers include an end-of-campaign report with recommendations for next steps.
