__Full-Service & Hybrid Publishing Partnership__

__What is the difference between Full-Service and Hybrid-Assisted publishing?__

In Full-Service Self-Publishing, BookCraft manages production, distribution, and growth while you retain full ownership and rights, and you fund production. In Hybrid-Assisted Publishing, BookCraft subsidizes a portion of production and launch costs in exchange for a defined revenue share. Full-Service suits authors who want maximum control; Hybrid suits authors who want to preserve capital and share risk with a commercially engaged partner.

__Do I keep the rights to my books?__

Yes. Both models are built around you retaining ownership of your work. The difference is in how production costs and revenue are shared, not in who owns the books. Exact terms — including any revenue share under the Hybrid model — are documented transparently in your agreement before anything moves forward.

__How do you decide which model to recommend?__

We start with a title-by-title review of your catalog's performance. The recommendation depends on the catalog's track record, your goals, your appetite for funding production versus sharing revenue, and whether active institutional or specialty sales are part of the plan. We present a clear comparison so the decision is yours.

__Is this only for authors with an existing catalog?__

It is strongest for authors with an existing catalog or backlist, because the model is built around long-term growth across multiple titles. Authors with a single title or in-development books can still partner with us — in-development titles can launch into an established backlist for added momentum.

__How is a partnership different from buying individual services?__

Individual services solve a specific need — a cover, an edit, a formatting job. A partnership is an ongoing relationship across the life of your books: catalog management, distribution, backlist optimization, new launches, and growth, under a single agreed model with regular reporting.

__When will I see specific costs and revenue terms?__

We prefer to settle scope, production level, and growth ambition first, because those decisions shape the economics. The full structure — costs, any revenue share, distribution support, and responsibilities — is walked through transparently before you commit to anything.
