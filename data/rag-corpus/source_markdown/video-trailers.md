# Video Book Trailer

***Bring Your Story to the Screen***

***Turn readers into viewers with cinematic storytelling.***

In the digital age, video is one of the most effective marketing channels — and that includes book marketing. BookCraft's book video trailer service creates captivating short trailers that bring your story to life on screen. Imagine a movie-style trailer for your book: dynamic text, imagery, music, and optional voiceover — all conveying your narrative's mood and hook in 30 to 120 seconds. These trailers are perfect for social media (Instagram Reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts), your author website, Amazon A+ Content, retailer book pages, virtual events, and pre-launch teaser campaigns.

Whether your book's vibe is dark and atmospheric or light and inspirational, we tailor the trailer's style to match. Our team handles everything from script and storyboard to final production. We use high-quality stock footage, custom illustrations, animations, original music, and professional voiceover to ensure your trailer hooks viewers in the first 5 seconds and leaves them eager to read your book. A well-produced trailer conveys book mood in ways static text and cover images alone can't — and gives you highly shareable content for promotion.

## Service Tiers

Video trailers are priced by **tier × duration**. Pick a tier that matches the production value you need and a duration that matches your distribution use case.

## What We Offer

- **Concept development** — We work with you to identify the trailer's hook, key emotional beats, and visual style. The strongest trailers communicate the book's *mood* in seconds, not the plot.

- **Script & storyboard** — Tight, evocative copy paired with a shot-by-shot storyboard. We aim for trailer copy that could be quoted in marketing — short, punchy, mysterious.

- **Visual production** — Stock footage and imagery, custom illustrations, motion graphics, 2D animation, kinetic typography. For Premium and Cinematic tiers, we can commission original artwork and custom character animation.

- **Voiceover** — Professional voice talent or AI-generated voiceover (Premium+ uses human talent). Voice direction handled by our producers.

- **Original music** — Licensed tracks (Premium and below), original composition (Premium and Cinematic). Music selection significantly affects trailer mood — we match score to genre.

- **Sound design** — Foley, atmospheric sound, transition stings, and sound effects matched to visual beats.

- **Multiple aspect ratios** — Same trailer delivered in 16:9 (YouTube, websites), 9:16 (TikTok, Reels, Stories), 1:1 (Instagram feed, Twitter), and 4:5 (Instagram feed optimized).

- **Subtitles & localization** — Open-caption subtitles for accessibility and silent-autoplay social, plus translation/localization for international markets.

- **Platform-optimized exports** — Different platforms have different specs (codec, bitrate, frame rate, max length). We deliver to spec for each platform you'll use.

## What Influences Cost & Timeline

Tier and duration set the base. These factors can shift price and time:

- **Visual style** — Stock-footage-driven is fastest; custom illustration takes more time; original animation takes the most
- **Animation level** — Static text on imagery vs. full motion graphics vs. character animation
- **Voiceover type** — None, AI voice, professional voice talent
- **Music type** — Stock royalty-free, licensed, custom-composed
- **Sound design level** — Music-only vs. full sound design with foley
- **Text/title animation** — Basic vs. advanced kinetic typography
- **Color grading level** — Standard correction vs. cinematic color treatment
- **Visual effects level** — Simple transitions vs. compositing, particle effects, advanced VFX
- **Illustration style** — Quantity and complexity of any custom illustration
- **Aspect ratios** — Standard 16:9 only vs. multiple platform formats
- **Platform optimization** — Single platform vs. cross-platform variant export

## Our Production Process

1. **Discovery & consultation.** Initial consultation to understand the book's genre, theme, target audience, and your vision for the trailer. We establish goals (launch announcement, ongoing marketing, retailer page, social ads), timeline, and creative direction.

2. **Script & storyboard development.** We craft a brief, engaging trailer script (or refine one you've drafted) and create a shot-by-shot storyboard or scene outline. The script highlights your book's hook, tone, and core appeal — without spoilers. We get your approval on script and storyboard before production begins.

3. **Visual production.** We create or source all visual assets — custom illustrations, animations, stock footage, text graphics, the book cover. Visual style is matched to your book's mood (dramatic visuals for fantasy, quick-cut imagery for thrillers, tender lighting for memoirs). For animated trailers, character design and animation begin here.

4. **Voiceover & sound design.** Voiceover is recorded (if your trailer uses one) — either by a professional voice actor, by you (if you want to narrate your own), or by AI for Essential-tier projects. Music is selected or composed; sound effects are designed to match visual beats.

5. **Editing, review & revisions.** All elements come together in editing. We assemble the trailer, refine pacing, color-grade, and add finishing audio. We share a draft with you for feedback. You comment on pacing, imagery, text, and tone. We revise — typically one or two rounds for Essential, more rounds at higher tiers — until the trailer is right.

6. **Final delivery & optimization.** Once approved, we export the trailer in all needed formats, optimized for each platform's technical specifications. You receive ready-to-upload files for YouTube, TikTok, Instagram, Facebook, and your website. We can also handle the upload itself if you'd like.

## Technical Specifications

- **Video formats:** MP4 (H.264) at 1080p as standard; 4K available for Premium and Cinematic tiers.
- **Aspect ratios:** 16:9 (YouTube, web embed) standard; 9:16 vertical (TikTok, Reels, YouTube Shorts), 1:1 square (Instagram feed), 4:5 (Instagram feed optimized) available as add-ons.
- **Audio:** 44.1kHz stereo, properly leveled (-14 LUFS for streaming platforms; -6dB peak for safety).
- **Captions:** Open captions (burned-in) for autoplay-with-sound-off social use, or closed captions (SRT files) for accessibility.
- **Platform specs:** YouTube up to 4K, Instagram Feed 9:16 or 4:5 max 60s, TikTok max 10min (but ~15-60s optimal), Twitter max 2:20.
- **Source files:** Project files (Premiere/After Effects) available on request.

## Common Questions

**What is included in your video trailer creation service?**
Our trailer service includes concept development, script and storyboard, visual asset creation or sourcing, voiceover (in tiers that include it), music selection or composition, sound design, editing, color grading, and final export in your needed platform formats. Revision rounds are included per your tier.

**Will the trailer include voiceover narration?**
Yes if you want it. Essential tier uses AI-generated voiceover or no voiceover (text-only). Professional tier uses curated AI voice or basic voice talent. Premium and Cinematic use professional voice actors with directed performances. You can also record your own voiceover with our coaching, especially for memoirs and personal-brand titles.

**Do you help with distributing the trailer on platforms like YouTube?**
Upload assistance is available — we can post the trailer to your YouTube, Vimeo, and social channels on your behalf. For ongoing distribution and ad campaigns built around the trailer, see our Marketing & Promotion service which handles paid video ads.

**Can you create trailers for specific platforms like Amazon A+ Content or Instagram?**
Yes. Each platform has different specs (aspect ratio, length, codec). We can deliver platform-specific cuts of the same trailer or design platform-native versions from the start. Amazon A+ Content allows up to 3 video modules per book detail page; Instagram Reels max at 90s.

**Do you create trailers for specific genres like romance or sci-fi?**
Absolutely — genre matters. Romance trailers benefit from emotional imagery and warm color grading; sci-fi trailers want sharp typography and atmospheric sound; thrillers need quick cuts and tension; literary fiction often goes more poetic and atmospheric. We tailor every aspect — pacing, music, visual style, color treatment — to genre conventions while finding distinctive elements unique to your book.

**Can the trailer include statistics or factual information for nonfiction books?**
Yes. Non-fiction trailers often lean on statistics, expert quotes, and clear value propositions ("the book that explains why..."). The script and visual approach differs from fiction trailers — more text-driven, more direct in messaging — but the production process is the same.

**Can the trailer highlight key plot points without giving away spoilers?**
Yes — and we recommend it. The strongest trailers communicate mood and intrigue, not plot. We help identify the "hook moments" that pique interest without revealing what happens. Good trailer copy creates a question in the viewer's mind that the book answers.

**Can you create trailers with interactive storytelling elements?**
Limited. True interactivity requires platforms that support branching video (some YouTube, ad platforms with conditional logic). For most distribution, "interactive" elements mean things like clickable end cards (CTAs to retailer pages) which are platform features rather than video editing.

**Can you create trailers that blend animation and live-action?**
Yes — Professional tier and above support mixed media. Live-action stock footage with animated graphic overlays is common; full character animation with live-action backgrounds is more involved (Premium tier). For specialized productions involving original live-action shoots, scope as a custom project.

**Can the trailer incorporate testimonials from influencers or reviewers?**
Yes. Pre-launch trailers can incorporate "advance praise" quotes from reviewers, blurbs from established authors, or testimonials from beta readers. Post-launch trailers can include reader reviews and bookstagrammer endorsements. Quotes need to be cleared with the source for use.
