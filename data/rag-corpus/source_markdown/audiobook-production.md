## What Influences Cost & Timeline

The base PFH rate covers a standard production. The following factors can shift price up (more complex/premium) or timeline out (more recording days, more post-production):

- **Narrator level** — Essential through Studio talent (the tier dimension above)
- **Voice requirements** — Single voice vs multiple distinct character voices
- **Accent/language complexity** — Single accent vs regional accents vs full multilingual
- **Recording environment** — Home studio vs professional studio
- **Audio quality level** — Standard vs broadcast vs cinema-quality mastering
- **Mastering level** — Platform-compliant vs advanced multi-platform optimization
- **Sound design level** — None, light ambience, full sound design with music
- **Music & scoring** — None, intro/outro stings, full original score
- **Book type difficulty** — Standard prose vs heavy dialogue vs technical/specialized vocabulary

## Our Production Process

1. **Voice casting & sample auditions.** We discuss your audiobook vision — desired tone, accent, pacing — and provide a curated selection of narrator audition samples reading from your manuscript or a similar passage. You choose the narrator who matches your book's voice.

2. **Narration planning & script prep.** Once the narrator is selected, we prepare the manuscript for recording. This includes a pronunciation guide for unique names and terminology, performance notes for tone shifts, and division into recording sessions.

3. **Studio recording & voice direction.** The narrator records under the guidance of a director or producer to maintain consistency in pacing, character voices, and emotional delivery. You can attend sessions virtually if you'd like to give real-time feedback.

4. **Editing, mixing & mastering.** Audio engineers edit out errors and pauses, balance levels, apply mastering, and ensure files meet platform technical requirements (RMS, peak, noise floor). Each chapter file opens and closes cleanly with the right room tone.

5. **Review & revisions.** You receive completed chapter files for review. One round of minor revisions is included — the narrator re-records small portions if needed for accuracy or tone.

6. **Final delivery & platform submission.** You receive a complete set of chapter files in MP3 and/or WAV formats. We can also handle ACX submission, Apple Books upload, and other platform-specific delivery on your behalf.

## Technical Specifications

- **File formats:** MP3 (192kbps or 256kbps CBR for ACX) and WAV (44.1kHz/16-bit, mono or stereo) per platform requirements.
- **Audio standards:** ACX-compliant — RMS between -23dB and -18dB, peak ≤ -3dB, noise floor ≤ -60dB. Each file opens with 0.5–1s of room tone and closes with 1–5s.
- **Chapter structure:** One audio file per chapter (or per major section), with chapter title spoken at the start. Typical maximum file length 120 minutes per ACX rules.
- **Cover art:** Square cover image at 2400×2400 pixels minimum, 72 DPI, RGB. We can adapt your existing book cover (see add-on: Audiobook Cover Adaptation in cover design service) or design an audiobook-specific cover.
- **Distribution platforms:** ACX/Audible (US, UK, AU, FR, DE), Apple Books, Google Play Books, Spotify Audiobooks, Kobo, Storytel, Findaway Voices (which distributes to libraries via OverDrive, Hoopla, and BorrowBox).

## Common Questions

**What is included in your audiobook production service?**
Our audiobook production includes the entire pipeline: script preparation, narrator casting and auditions, professional studio recording (or coordination with the narrator's studio), audio editing for clarity and pacing, mastering to platform specifications, prooflistening against the manuscript, one round of revisions, and platform-ready file delivery.

**Can I choose the narrator for my audiobook?**
Yes. We curate 3–5 narrator samples specifically for your book based on your genre, tone, and any author preferences. You listen to short audition clips and approve the narrator before any full recording begins. If none of the initial samples feel right, we'll source additional auditions until we find the right voice.

**Can I narrate my audiobook with your assistance?**
Yes. We support author-narrated audiobooks with pre-production coaching (pacing, breathing, microphone technique), studio guidance, full post-production, and platform delivery. Especially common for memoirs, business books, and thought-leadership titles where the author's voice is itself a feature.

**How do you ensure high-quality narration?**
Our narrators are vetted professionals with audiobook-specific experience. We match narrator to genre (e.g., dramatic voice for thrillers, warm tone for romance, authoritative for non-fiction), provide a pronunciation guide for unique terms, and have a director or producer oversee recording sessions to maintain consistency in pacing, characterization, and emotional tone.

**What formats will the final audiobook be delivered in?**
Industry-standard MP3 (192–256kbps for ACX) and/or WAV (44.1kHz/16-bit) files, organized one per chapter. We deliver in whatever format your target platform requires. ACX-compliant masters meet RMS, peak, and noise-floor specifications on the first submission.

**Can you produce audiobooks for platforms like Audible, Apple Books, or Spotify?**
Yes. We produce to the technical specifications of every major audiobook platform: ACX/Audible, Apple Books, Google Play Books, Spotify Audiobooks, Kobo, Storytel, and Findaway Voices for library distribution. We can also handle the platform submission process on your behalf as part of our publishing service.

**How long does the audiobook production process take?**

Timeline is calculated by our central Pricing & Timeline engine based on your project parameters. Ask me for an estimate and I'll return a precise range.

**Are narrators trained to handle accents or dialects?**
Yes. Many of our narrators specialize in regional accents (British, Irish, Scottish, US Southern, Australian, etc.) or specific dialects. For multilingual books, we engage native speakers for foreign-language inserts via the Foreign Language Sections add-on.

**Do you provide guidance on audiobook length and pacing?**
Yes. As part of script prep, we estimate finished length from your word count (roughly word_count ÷ 9,300 = finished hours) and advise on pacing based on genre conventions. We may flag passages where the original prose suits visual reading more than aural, so you can decide whether to adapt or have the narrator handle the passage with extra performance care.

**How do you manage projects with multiple voice actors?**
Multi-voice productions use the "Multiple Narrator Coordination" add-on. We assign a production lead who coordinates schedules across narrators, maintains tone consistency through detailed character bibles, and manages the technical work of stitching multi-narrator chapters into seamless audio. Common configurations: one primary narrator + one for opposite-gender dialogue (dual-POV novels), or full ensemble cast for dramatized productions.
