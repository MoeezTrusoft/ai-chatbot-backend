__Translation & Foreign-Rights / Localization__

__Do you translate the book in-house with software?__

No. We coordinate professional human literary translators who work into their native language. Machine translation is not used as the final product for literary work because it misses voice, tone, and cultural nuance — the things that make a translated book read well.

__Should I license my translation rights or self-publish localized editions?__

It depends on your goals, the market, and the offers available. Licensing to an established foreign publisher brings their distribution and local expertise; self-publishing localized editions keeps more control and revenue with you. We help you weigh both and keep your rights clearly mapped either way. Note that we are not a law firm — final contract terms should be reviewed by your own attorney.

__How do you choose the right languages and markets?__

We assess where your book has the strongest audience potential based on genre, subject, and any existing international interest, and prioritize the markets most likely to reward the investment rather than translating into everything at once.

__Is metadata translated too?__

Metadata is localized, not just translated. Titles, descriptions, keywords, and categories are recreated for each market so the book is discoverable and positioned correctly where it is sold — a direct translation of a title or keyword often performs poorly.

__Will the cover and interior change?__

Sometimes. Some markets expect different visual conventions, so we adapt the cover or interior where it helps the book succeed, while keeping your brand recognizable. Many editions need only the text and metadata localized.

__Who owns the translated editions and rights?__

You retain ownership of your work. We keep a clear record of which rights are licensed or self-published, in which territories and languages, and for how long, so nothing is lost or double-committed.
