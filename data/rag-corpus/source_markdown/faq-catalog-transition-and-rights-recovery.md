__Catalog Transition & Rights Recovery__

__My publisher is closing. Can you help me keep my books available?__

Yes. This is exactly what the service is for. We manage the operational transition of your catalog into your own publishing operation — recovering rights and files, migrating platform accounts, rebuilding metadata, and reconciling royalties — so your titles stay available and your readers and reviews are preserved.

__Will I lose my sales history, reviews, or rankings?__

We plan a continuity-preserving migration so that, wherever the platforms allow it, your listings keep their history rather than being rebuilt as brand-new entries. Some platforms have limits on what can transfer, and we are transparent about those cases, but preserving continuity is a primary goal of the process.

__Do I need a lawyer, and is this a legal dispute?__

The service is designed to be handled cooperatively, not as a legal demand. Where formal language is used, it is for clarity and record-keeping. That said, we are not a law firm and do not provide legal advice; for questions about the interpretation of your contracts you should consult your own attorney. We organize and act on the rights information; we do not adjudicate it.

__What do you need from my former publisher?__

Typically nine categories of information: rights and contracts, platform accounts and credentials, ISBNs and metadata, production assets, editorial and marketing materials, financial and royalty records, third-party contacts, sub-license and foreign-rights arrangements, and physical inventory or distribution notes. They can provide these in any order, and “no longer available” is a complete answer for any item.

__What does BookCraft handle versus what I have to do?__

BookCraft handles all new account setups, ISBN work, metadata rebuilds, file migration and storage, and communications with retailers and distributors. You provide authorization and any information only you hold, and countersign a short closing acknowledgment at the end. The day-to-day operational steps do not require your involvement.

__Is my information kept confidential?__

Yes. Credentials, banking details, contracts, and financial records are treated as strictly confidential, used solely to continue your catalog, and held in secure, access-controlled storage. We are happy to sign a mutual non-disclosure agreement if that helps.

__Can you also help me grow the catalog after the transition?__

Yes. Once your titles are under your control, we can refresh covers, descriptions, and positioning, coordinate a re-launch, and apply a title-by-title growth strategy — and you can layer on any of our other services as needed.
