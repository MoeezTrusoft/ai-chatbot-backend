# Publishing & Distribution

## What Influences Cost & Timeline

- **Distribution channels** — More channels = more setup time but more reach
- **Print format** — eBook-only is fastest; hardcover requires the most file prep
- **Trim size complexity** — Standard sizes (5×8, 6×9) move faster than custom trims
- **Color interior** — Significantly increases print cost; also requires color-profile validation
- **eBook formats** — EPUB-only is standard; additional formats (legacy MOBI, PDF) are extra
- **DRM requirements** — DRM-free is faster and works on more retailers; DRM-locked is required for some channels
- **Accessibility level** — Basic accessibility (alt text, semantic structure) is standard; full WCAG-compliant requires additional work
- **Distribution level** — Wide distribution opens more channels but more setup work

## Our Publishing Process

1. **Manuscript finalization & file prep.** We confirm your manuscript and cover are publication-ready (formatted, proofread, designed). Final QC pass on files.

2. **Metadata preparation.** Title, subtitle, author bio, description, BISAC categories (up to 3 primary), keywords (up to 7 per book — and these are critical for discovery), audience targeting, age range, language. We optimize each retailer's allowed metadata fields.

3. **File conversion.** EPUB validation for eBook retailers, print-ready PDF generation with proper bleed and trim for print, MOBI generation for legacy Kindle if needed.

4. **ISBN assignment.** Decision on Bowker-purchased ISBN (recommended for serious authors — same ISBN across all retailers) vs. free retailer ISBNs (KDP-issued, which lock you to KDP). One ISBN per format (separate for eBook, paperback, hardcover, audiobook).

5. **Platform setup.** Account creation or existing-account integration for KDP, IngramSpark, Apple Books, Kobo, Barnes & Noble Press, etc. Banking and tax setup (W-9/W-8BEN for international authors).

6. **File upload & validation.** Upload files to each platform; fix any format-validation errors that come back; resubmit. We manage the back-and-forth with platform support if issues arise.

7. **Quality assurance & proof orders.** Order a physical proof copy (or two) for print formats. Inspect for printing issues, cover quality, interior layout. Authorize for sale only after proof approval.

8. **Pre-launch preparation.** Set the launch date and synchronize across platforms. Pre-order setup for retailers that support it. Final metadata, pricing, and category checks.

9. **Launch.** Books go live on the scheduled date. We verify availability across all platforms, check metadata display, confirm pricing and category placement.

10. **Post-launch & ongoing management.** Monitor first-month performance (sales, ranking, reviews). Address any platform issues that arise. Premium tier includes quarterly catalog reviews and metadata refreshes.

## Common Questions

**What is included in your publishing service package?**
Publishing packages include manuscript and cover file prep, metadata optimization, ISBN guidance, platform setup (varies by tier), file upload and validation, proof copy ordering, launch coordination, and post-launch support. Higher tiers add multi-platform distribution, audiobook setup, library distribution, and ongoing catalog management.

**What are BookCraft's primary distribution channels?**
Amazon KDP (eBook and print), IngramSpark (wide retail and library), Apple Books, Kobo, Barnes & Noble Press, Google Play Books for eBooks; ACX/Audible, Apple Books Audio, Spotify Audiobooks, Findaway Voices for audiobooks; OverDrive, Hoopla, BorrowBox for library distribution. Through Draft2Digital aggregation we also reach 30+ smaller retailers.

**Can you publish in multiple languages?**
Yes. Each language version is published separately (different ISBN, different metadata, different retailer accounts in some cases). We can coordinate translation through our network or work with translations you've already commissioned.

**What happens if a platform rejects my book submission?**
Rejections happen — usually for fixable reasons (formatting issues, metadata violations, content classification). We handle the resubmission process: identify the rejection reason, fix the underlying issue (file fix, metadata adjustment, content review), and resubmit. We're familiar with each platform's quirks and rejection patterns.

**Do authors retain the copyright to their work under a BookCraft contract?**
Yes. You retain 100% of your copyright, 100% of your royalties, and 100% of your rights. BookCraft acts as a service provider, not a publisher claiming rights. Your books are published in your name, with copyright in your name, and you can move them, modify them, or unpublish them at any time.

**How are royalties calculated?**
Royalties are paid by retailers directly to you (we don't intermediate), so the calculations are theirs:
- **KDP eBook:** Tiered royalty rate depending on price band and territory — see KDP's published royalty schedule for current rates
- **KDP Print:** List price minus printing cost minus retailer commission
- **IngramSpark:** List price minus printing cost (with adjustable wholesale discount)
- **Apple Books:** Flat per-sale royalty — see Apple Books' current schedule
- **Audible/ACX:** Different rates for exclusive vs. non-exclusive distribution — see ACX's current royalty terms
The retailers pay you directly via direct deposit, and you handle your own taxes.

**Who owns the ISBN?**
You do. Whether you purchase your own ISBN through Bowker (recommended — gives you imprint flexibility) or use a free retailer-issued ISBN (which locks you to that retailer), the ISBN is registered to you as the publisher. We don't claim publisher rights or imprint ownership.

**Do you offer bundled editing, design, and publishing packages?**
Yes — many clients bundle services for end-to-end publication. Bundles get coordinated handoffs (e.g., the cover designer knows the trim size before formatting starts), unified project management, and often discounted pricing vs. ordering services individually. The chatbot can scope a custom bundle.

**Do you provide royalty calculation and management services?**
Royalties are paid directly by retailers to you, so most authors don't need management services. For authors at higher volume or with complex royalty arrangements (multi-author projects, ghostwritten royalty splits), we offer royalty tracking and statement compilation as a custom service.

**Do you assist with crowdfunding campaigns for book publishing?**
Yes. Kickstarter and Indiegogo are valid launch paths, especially for special-edition and illustrated books. We coordinate with our marketing service for the campaign itself; publishing handles the post-funding fulfillment (file prep, retailer setup, fulfillment-service coordination).

**Will you assist with book registration in catalogs like Bowker or CIP?**
Yes. Bowker registration (where you buy your ISBN and register your book in the Books in Print database) is part of our metadata service. Library of Congress CIP (Cataloging-in-Publication) data is available for traditionally-published books with established imprints; for self-published books, PCN (Preassigned Control Number) is the alternative — we handle that filing.

**Can you assist with publishing free or promotional books?**
Yes. KDP Select free promotion days, BookFunnel for newsletter-magnet free books, and permanent-free Series-Book-1 strategies are all standard in our marketing playbook. Setup happens through our publishing service; promotion runs through our marketing service.
